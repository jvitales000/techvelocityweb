Project Overview

The Clinical AI Platform That Keeps PHI Where It Belongs — With You.

Healthcare AI has a fundamental tension: the most valuable insights come from patient data, but that data carries the highest compliance burden. Most platforms force a choice between capability and safety. AIVault eliminates that trade-off.

Built on Azure's healthcare cloud infrastructure with a FHIR-first data model, AIVault routes all clinical data through a Privacy Gate before it ever reaches an AI engine. Identity and protected health information stay in a secured identity stream. Only de-identified, tokenized clinical content enters the AI processing zone — by architectural design, not policy alone.

Platform Pillars

Three Principles. One Platform.

Clinical Intelligence

AI models trained on de-identified clinical data deliver diagnostic support, risk stratification, and outcomes prediction — without ever accessing raw patient identifiers. Insights are re-linked to patient context after processing, in a HIPAA-controlled environment.

Privacy-First Design

PHI de-identification is not a feature — it is the architecture. Every data flow passes through the AHDS Privacy Gate before reaching any AI engine. Token mapping, pseudonymization, and differential privacy are built into the pipeline at the infrastructure level.

Unified FHIR Core

All inputs — video assessments, spiral drawings, clinical notes, EHR data — are normalized to FHIR R4 resources. Outputs are returned as FHIR-compliant bundles, making integration with any modern EHR system seamless and standards-based.

The Challenge

The Problem & Our Solution

The Problem

Healthcare AI Demands Access to the Most Protected Data in Existence

Clinical AI models need patient data to be useful — but HIPAA, state regulations, and patient trust demand that data stay protected. Most healthcare AI platforms store or transmit PHI to third-party models, creating massive compliance exposure. Security teams say no. Clinical teams lose the tools they need.

Our Solution

AIVault Separates Identity From Intelligence — By Design

The AHDS Privacy Gate splits every data stream at the source. Protected identity data stays in an encrypted, access-controlled identity store. De-identified clinical content flows to Azure AI Foundry for processing. The AI never sees who the patient is. The clinician sees the full picture. Compliance is architectural, not procedural.

Architecture

End-to-End Clinical AI Pipeline

1

AHDS Privacy Gate

All clinical inputs enter the Azure Health Data Services Privacy Gate. PHI is identified, tokenized, and routed to the secure identity stream. De-identified content continues to AI.

2

Azure AI Foundry

De-identified clinical content is processed by specialized AI models — NLP, computer vision, risk scoring — entirely within your Azure environment. Zero PHI in this zone.

3

4

Dual React Interfaces

FHIR-backed React applications surface insights to clinicians and patients. All interface queries go through SMART on FHIR authorization — role-based, audited, and compliant.

Privacy Gate

Two Streams. One Unbreakable Boundary.

The AHDS Privacy Gate splits every clinical data flow into two parallel streams. They never merge inside the AI processing zone.

Secure Identity Stream

Protected Health Information

  • Patient name, DOB, MRN, contact data
  • Insurance identifiers and payer codes
  • Location and demographic identifiers
  • Biometric data and facial recognition markers
  • Stored in AHDS with RBAC and audit logging
  • Access requires authenticated clinical authorization
  • Never routed to AI model endpoints
De-Identified AI Stream

Clinical Content for AI Processing

  • Tokenized clinical notes (no patient identifiers)
  • Video motion data (no facial or biometric markers)
  • Spiral drawing vectors (no patient metadata)
  • Structured FHIR clinical observations
  • Processed entirely in Azure AI Foundry
  • Outputs returned as anonymous FHIR observations
  • Re-linked to patient identity only post-processing
Zero PHI AI Zone

Three AI Engines. Zero Patient Identity.

All AI processing occurs in an isolated Azure environment where PHI cannot exist by architectural constraint — not just policy.

Clinical NLP Engine

Processes de-identified clinical notes to extract diagnoses, medication references, symptom patterns, and clinical indicators. Trained on healthcare corpora with medical terminology understanding. Outputs structured FHIR Observation and Condition resources.

Computer Vision Engine

Analyzes video motion data and spiral drawing vectors for motor dysfunction markers, tremor quantification, and behavioral patterns. Operates entirely on anonymized movement data — no facial recognition, no biometric identification.

Risk Stratification Engine

Aggregates NLP and vision outputs to generate clinical risk scores, disease progression indicators, and care prioritization flags. All outputs are FHIR-compliant risk assessment resources, ready for EHR integration.

Compliance

Built for the Most Regulated Environment in Healthcare

AIVault's compliance posture is not a checklist — it is the architecture. Every design decision, from data routing to access control to audit logging, was made with HIPAA, HITECH, and Azure Health Data Services requirements as first-order constraints.

✓

PHI De-identification at Source

HIPAA Safe Harbor and Expert Determination methods applied before any AI processing. No PHI enters the AI zone.

✓

End-to-End Encryption

Data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3) throughout the entire pipeline, including identity and AI streams.

✓

Role-Based Access Control

SMART on FHIR authorization enforces clinician, admin, and patient roles. Access to PHI requires explicit authorization scopes.

✓

Complete Audit Trail

Every data access, AI query, and result delivery is logged with immutable timestamps. Ready for HIPAA audit, OCR investigation, and payer review.
